JW Auctions: My E-Commerce/ Auctioning Website

Time Of Completion: 2023
Technologies Used: PHP, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Git/ Bitbucket, Obsidian for documentation, MySQL database

This was my A-Level Computer Science Coursework. Following marking of this coursework, I was told that my coursework was likely the school's highest ever computer science coursework mark.

This is by far my largest non-work-based project, spanning over 12 different page types. On top of this, I made all deadlines and it would be deployable with some more formal unit tests and financial infrastructure integration. However, both of these aspects were outside of the marking criteria.

I was marked for my scripting (JavaScript and PHP), rather than the visual appeal of the website. I acknowledge this area of the website needs more work and I hope to revisit the project soon.

Documentation and code available upon legitimate request, if you would like to see feel free to send me an email.

University of Manchester Computational Thinking Page (Coursework)

Time Of Completion: 2024
Technologies Used: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, LaTeX

This was my first group project in the world of software and it was completed over the course of roughly 3 weeks. We organised weekly meetings which all 3 members of the team were present for. I took regular lead of the development side of house whilst others found themselves better at designing the website and filling the pages with content.

I also undertook writing a report document up in LaTex. This is now my go-to for note taking and the report was marked fairly well. We looked carefully into accessibility, ease of use and formatting for focus retention for our users.

We then concluded the project with a presentation. During this presentation, I covered a highly abstracted view of the technical aspects whilst the others discussed design aspects and the group's organisational structure.
